Conduction velocity

The action potential shape of cells on an individual level can affect how an impulse propagates across multiple cells on a macroscopic level. One important macroscopic feature is conduction velocity. (The main determinant of conduction velocity is the speed of the upstroke of the action potential of individual cells - the slower the upstroke, the slower conduction velocity.)

Let's just focus on the macroscopic level from now on. In these simulations, we have the option of two conduction velocities - normal (the default) and slow:

  1. Start by clicking on the left-most cell to depolarise it and observe the impulse propagate across the two paths.
  2. Open the tools section. The currently active button in this section determines what happens when you click on any cell on the grid. If you ever need to know what a certain button does, you can click on the help button to activate tooltips.
  3. Select the button to activate slow conduction velocity
  4. Click and drag to select a few of the cells in the top path. You will see a marker on these cells to indicate their conduction velocity is slow.
  5. Select the button to activate depolarisation, then click on the left-most cell to depolarise it.
  6. Note the difference in impulse propagation between the two paths.
